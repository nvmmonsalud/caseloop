# Build Week demo script

Target runtime: 2:25–2:40. The final upload must remain under three minutes.

## 0:00–0:16 — The learning problem

**Visual:** CaseFlow title, then the landing page and demo role selector.

**Narration:** “Case teaching works when students arrive with an independent, evidence-backed point of view. But preparation often collapses into summaries, while faculty face a pile of repetitive submissions. CaseFlow turns that preparation into judgment—and turns cohort reasoning into a better classroom discussion.”

## 0:16–0:58 — Student preparation

**Visual:** Enter the Hikari Foods student journey. Show objectives, recommendation, source selection, uncertainty, and the Socratic challenge.

**Narration:** “This is the fictional Hikari Foods market-entry case. Maya starts with her own recommendation, cites case evidence, and names what she still does not know. The AI asks before it tells: it pressures one assumption, introduces a competing stakeholder, and preserves the student’s responsibility to decide. Sources stay visible, and facts, assumptions, and inference remain distinct.”

## 0:58–1:28 — Commitment and brief

**Visual:** Choose the joint venture, add rationale, risk, mitigation, and confidence; reveal the preparation brief and reflection comparison.

**Narration:** “Maya then commits to an entry mode, rationale, risk, mitigation, and confidence. CaseFlow turns that reasoning into a concise preparation brief with evidence, counterargument, open question, and source links. After class, reflection compares what changed in her reasoning without pretending to grade her judgment.”

## 1:28–1:58 — Faculty intelligence

**Visual:** Switch to faculty. Show completion, average confidence, position mix, cohort insight, anonymized arguments, and the editable 60/90-minute planner.

**Narration:** “Faculty see patterns, not surveillance: completion, confidence, recommendation mix, evidence use, misconceptions, and anonymized representative arguments. These figures are computed from twelve synthetic responses, not invented by the model. Small cohorts are suppressed, and the teaching plan remains editable and faculty-controlled.”

## 1:58–2:23 — GPT-5.6 inside the product

**Visual:** Architecture diagram and five workflow labels.

**Narration:** “Inside CaseFlow, GPT‑5.6 powers five narrow workflows through Vercel AI Gateway: Socratic coaching, brief generation, cohort analysis, discussion planning, and reflection comparison. Each has a dedicated server prompt, source IDs, a Zod-validated structured output, bounded timeouts, and a deterministic fallback. There is no automated high-stakes grading.”

## 2:23–2:42 — Codex and close

**Visual:** Codex contribution card, test evidence, then return to the CaseFlow mark and tagline.

**Narration:** “We used Codex as our engineering partner to shape the product, implement the complete workflow, create synthetic data, build the InsForge persistence and privacy controls, write tests, and verify deployment. CaseFlow is a narrow but complete wedge toward an AI-native learning system. Think before the room speaks.”
