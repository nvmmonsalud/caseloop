# Three-minute demo script

## 0:00–0:20 — Problem and product

“Case teaching works when students arrive with independent judgment. Today, students often bring summaries and faculty receive a pile of repetitive prep. CaseFlow turns preparation into evidence, commitment, challenge, and a better classroom discussion.” Show the landing page and enter the student demo.

## 0:20–1:20 — Student journey

Show Hikari Foods and its objectives. Open the workspace; note that every entity and figure is fictional. Enter a recommendation, cite `[S2]`, and name an uncertainty. Continue: “The AI asks before it tells. It tests one assumption and introduces a competing stakeholder.” At the checkpoint choose joint venture, add rationale, risk, mitigation, and confidence. Generate the brief. Point to evidence, assumptions, counterargument, open question, and sources. Briefly show reflection comparison.

## 1:20–2:15 — Faculty insight and planner

Switch roles. Show 12 of 24 completed, 74% average confidence, and the recommendation mix. Open cohort insight. “These numbers are computed from 12 seeded responses—never fabricated by the model.” Show evidence frequency, the outlet-access misconception, overlooked integration evidence, and anonymized arguments. Open the planner, switch 60 to 90 minutes, edit a prompt, and save.

## 2:15–2:40 — AI architecture and safeguards

Show the architecture diagram. “Five GPT-5.6 workflows run through Vercel AI Gateway with dedicated server prompts, source IDs, and Zod outputs. Provider, timeout, or schema failures activate the same-shaped validated fallback. Facts, student assumptions, and AI inference stay distinct. There is no high-stakes grading; faculty control released content.”

## 2:40–3:00 — Impact and vision

“CaseFlow can measure completion, evidence coverage, confidence/support mismatch, and faculty synthesis time. This is a narrow but complete wedge. The larger vision is an AI-native LMS organized around learning loops—not folders, forms, and a chatbot.” Return to: “Think before the room speaks.”
